I am currently a Ph.D. Software Engineering candidate at McMaster University under the advisement of Drs. Mark Lawford and Alan Wassyng. My research is done in collaboration with the Electrified Powertain group at Fiat Chrysler Automobiles (FCA). I also work part-time in industry as a Powertrain Engineer in the Electrified Powertrain group at FCA, specifically on model-based software/controls, their development processes, and architecture.

I am a member of the McMaster Centre for Software Certification (McSCert), as well as part of Dr. Ali Emadi's Canada Excellence Research Chair in Hybrid Powertrain group at the McMaster Institute for Automotive Research and Technology (MacAUTO). I am a member of the embedded software research team (profile) on the Automotive Partnership Canada - "Leadership in Automotive Powertrain" (APC-LEAP) project, which is a collaboration between McMaster University and FCA, for the development of next generation hybrid-electric drivetrains for vehicles.

My main research interests lie in the areas of model-based development, safety-critical systems, and software engineering design principles. I am particularly interested in information hiding, modularity, designing for change, and software architecture in the automotive industry.

I am a recipient of the NSERC Alexander Graham Bell Canada Graduate Scholarship - Doctoral (CGS D).
